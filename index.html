About Me

I am a 4th year PhD student at CU-ICAR where I pursue my PhD in Automotive Engineering. Working under my advisor, Dr. Yunyi Jia, I have worked on a US DOE Energy Efficient Mobility Systems project tasked with increasing the usage of pooled rideshare. My contribution to the project includes the design and simulation-based validation of fleet control algorithms to better address rider needs. Working with Argonne National Laboratory, I develop and deploy algorithms within the POLARIS Transportation System Simulation Tool. I spent summer of 2023 with the Argonne National Lab Team in Chicago, IL for a PhD Research Internship focusing on this research.

During my time at Clemson, I also have served as the General Manager of the Velo Fellow, a single location bar in downtown Greenville, SC. During my three years here, I have developed a music program, addressed costs through labor and goods impacted by COVID, and increased profitability significantly. I manage a staff of 19 personnel in the kitchen, bar, marketing, and entertainment areas of the business.

Prior to and during my PhD, I also completed my Master of Science in Operations Management through the University of Alabama's Manderson School of Business. During this degree, I focused heavily on scheduling, optimization, finance, and inventory management: all skills I have been able to apply functionally in my managerial and research roles.

Prior to my PhD, I was employed in a rotational development program at BMW Manufacturing in Greer, SC, where I spent time as a Process Specialist in the body hang-ons shop, focused on big data analytics to identify problem robotic cells and improve preemptive identification of problem cells. I also spent a rotation in pre-production functional validation, where I represented a team of specialists on behalf of the project.

I completed my Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Alabama in 2020. During undergrad, I completed three cooperative education rotations at BMW Manufacturing, in Greer, SC. During these rotations I served as a quality engineer in the assembly hall, with a focus on visual inspection systems and analytics. I also managed the logistic flow of vehicles through the Special Vehicle Operations center.

My other professional experience includes six months total of controls engineering internships at Premier Automation, in Pittsburgh, PA where I focused on mechanical design for bus bar linkages and scheduling problems.  I also managed the University of Alabama Outdoor Recreation Bicycle shop and designed and implemented a work-order ticketing system still in use today.

Finally, in my free time, I serve on two boards. I am a board member for the Sterling Land Trust, a non-profit affordable housing trust based in the historic Sterling Neighborhood that my wife and I call home. I also serve as the treasurer for the August Brook Home Owners Association in Berea, SC.
